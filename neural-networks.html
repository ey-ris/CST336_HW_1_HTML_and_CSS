What Are Neural Networks?

Neural networks are a series of algorithms that mimic the operations of a human brain to recognize relationships between vast amounts of data. They are the foundation of deep learning and are particularly useful for recognizing patterns in data.

How Neural Networks Work

A neural network consists of layers of interconnected nodes (neurons). Each connection has a weight that adjusts as learning proceeds. The network learns by adjusting these weights based on the errors it makes in its predictions.

Deep learning uses neural networks with many layers (hence "deep") to learn increasingly abstract features from the data.
