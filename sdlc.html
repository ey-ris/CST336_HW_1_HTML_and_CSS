Software Development Life Cycle Methodologies

Waterfall Model

The Waterfall model is a linear sequential approach where each phase must be completed before the next begins. It's simple to understand but lacks flexibility for changing requirements.

Phases: Requirements, Design, Implementation, Verification, Maintenance

Agile Methodology

Agile is an iterative approach that focuses on collaboration, customer feedback, and small, rapid releases. It adapts well to changing requirements.

Key principles: Individuals and interactions, working software, customer collaboration, responding to change

DevOps

DevOps combines software development (Dev) and IT operations (Ops) to shorten the development lifecycle while delivering features, fixes, and updates frequently.

Practices: Continuous integration, continuous delivery, infrastructure as code, monitoring
